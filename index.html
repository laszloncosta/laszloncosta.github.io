About Me

Hailing from the land of Padre Cícero, Juazeiro do Norte, I'm a professional nerd who decided one engineering degree wasn't enough, so I went ahead and collected all three: a B.S., an M.S., and the final boss, a Ph.D. from the Federal University of Ceará. My passion for science and telecommunications led me on a side quest as a researcher at GTEL, where I teamed up with Ericsson. The loot from that adventure was a special guest-star episode in Germany as a Ph.D. candidate. After my academic world tour, I settled in as a Senior Software Developer at Atlantico Institute, slinging C++ code for an HP project from 2022 to 2024. In 2024, I leveled up to IT Project Manager at Atlantico, where I now manage the quests instead of just coding them. When I'm not deep into projects, you can find me consuming an unhealthy amount of anime, movies, and games, or pondering the arcane arts of MIMO transceivers and convex optimization—because apparently, my brain never wants to rest.

Resume

I hold a B.S. degree in Computer Engineering, an M.S. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering, and a Doctor degree from the Federal University of Ceará (UFC), Brazil. My passion for technology and innovation led me to work as a researcher at the Wireless Telecommunications Research Group (GTEL) in Brazil, where I collaborated on research projects with Ericsson. My dedication and hard work earned me the opportunity to be a guest Ph.D. candidate at the Technische Universität Darmstadt in Germany. Later, I worked as a Senior Software Developer using C++ at Atlantico Institute, where I was part of a project with HP from 2022 to 2024. In 2024, I started working as an IT Project Manager at Atlantico Institute and I'm still enjoying my work there today. My research interests include MIMO transceiver design techniques, convex/integer optimization, interference management, and radio resource allocation.
