I am an Assistant Professor at the Federal University of Cariri (UFCA), Brazil. I hold a Ph.D. in Teleinformatics Engineering, as well as M.Sc. and B.Sc. degrees in engineering-related fields from the Federal University of Ceará (UFC).

My research interests are centered on wireless communications, with emphasis on radio resource management, optimization, machine learning for communication systems, and MU-MIMO. More broadly, I am interested in the design and evaluation of intelligent techniques for next-generation wireless networks, especially in 5G and 6G scenarios.

During my doctoral studies, I was affiliated with the Wireless Telecommunications Research Group (GTEL/UFC), where I worked on problems related to wireless systems in collaboration with industry. I also spent time as a visiting researcher at TU Darmstadt, Germany.

Before joining UFCA, I worked in the technology industry at the Atlântico Institute, first as a Senior Software Developer and later as an IT Project Manager. This experience strengthened my interest in bridging academic research and practical engineering development.

At UFCA, I am committed to teaching, research, and contributing to the development of technology and innovation in the Cariri region. I am particularly interested in building research initiatives that connect wireless networks, optimization, artificial intelligence, and real-world impact.

Feel free to explore my publications, projects, and recent activities on this website.
